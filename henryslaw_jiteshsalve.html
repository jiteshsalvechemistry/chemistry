HENRY'S LAW

Solubility of Gases in Liquids
S = KH × P
Interactive Simulation by Jitesh Salve
Statement of Henry's Law
"The solubility of a gas in a liquid is directly proportional to the pressure of the gas over the solution"
— William Henry, 1803 | Maharashtra HSC Board Chapter 2
S ∝ P
Solubility is proportional to Pressure
S = KH × P
S = Solubility of gas (mol L⁻¹)  |  P = Pressure of gas (bar)
KH = Henry's Law Constant (mol L⁻¹ bar⁻¹)
💡 The Simple Idea

Imagine a bottle of cold drink (soda). It is sealed with high pressure of CO₂ gas inside. Because of that high pressure, lots of CO₂ gas gets dissolved in the liquid. The moment you open the bottle — pressure drops — and all that dissolved gas escapes as bubbles! That's exactly Henry's Law.


More pressure → more gas dissolves. Less pressure → less gas dissolves.

Important Note on KH
📌 What is KH?

KH is the Henry's Law Constant. It depends on:
• The nature of the gas (CO₂, O₂, N₂, etc.)
• The temperature of the solution

A higher KH means more soluble gas at that pressure.

When P = 1 bar → S = KH × 1 = KH
So KH is the solubility of the gas when pressure = 1 bar.

Real Life Applications
🥤
Carbonated Drinks
CO₂ sealed under high pressure. Opens → pressure drops → bubbles!
🤿
Deep Sea Diving
High water pressure → N₂ dissolves in blood. Fast rise causes "bends"
🐟
Fish in Water
Fish breathe O₂ dissolved in water. More O₂ pressure = more dissolved O₂
🏔️
High Altitude
Low O₂ pressure at mountains → less O₂ in blood → breathlessness
Exceptions to Henry's Law
⚠️ Gases that do NOT obey Henry's Law: NH₃ (Ammonia) and CO₂ (Carbon dioxide) — because these gases react with water chemically. Since they form new compounds (NH₄OH and H₂CO₃), their actual solubility is much higher than what Henry's Law would predict.
📝 Board Exam Tip: "State and explain Henry's Law" is asked almost every year — 3 marks. Always write: Statement → Formula with meaning of each symbol → One real-life application → Exceptions.

Every Symbol Explained
S
Solubility of the Gas How much gas dissolves in 1 litre of liquid. Unit: mol L⁻¹ (moles per litre). More dissolved gas = higher S.
P
Pressure of Gas over Solution The pressure that the gas exerts on top of the liquid surface. Unit: bar. Higher P pushes more gas into the liquid.
KH
Henry's Law Constant The proportionality constant for a specific gas at a specific temperature. Unit: mol L⁻¹ bar⁻¹. It is numerically equal to the solubility when P = 1 bar. Different gas → different KH.
∝
"Is directly proportional to" S ∝ P means: if pressure doubles, solubility also doubles. They increase and decrease together.
mol L⁻¹
Moles per Litre (unit of S) This is the unit of solubility — how many moles of gas are dissolved in 1 litre of solution.
bar
Bar (unit of Pressure) Unit of gas pressure. 1 bar ≈ 1 atmosphere. Normal atmospheric pressure ≈ 1 bar.
🧠 Memory Trick:
Think of Henry's Law like a sponge under water. The more you press (more pressure), the more water (gas) it absorbs (dissolves). Release the press (lower pressure) — the water comes out (gas escapes as bubbles). Squeeze more = absorb more. That's Henry's Law!
⚗️ Important Formula Summary:
S = KH × P
KH = S ÷ P
P = S ÷ KH
